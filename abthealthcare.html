                Primary Care: This is usually the first point of contact for individuals seeking healthcare services. It involves general medical practitioners who 
                provide routine check-ups, basic treatment, and referrals to specialists if needed.
                Specialized Care: Specialized care involves healthcare professionals with advanced training in specific fields such as cardiology,oncology,neurology,
                etc. These professionals diagnose and treat complex medical conditions.
                Preventive Care: This focuses on preventing diseases or injuries before they occur.It includes vaccinations,screenings,health education,and lifestyle 
                modifications to promote overall well-being.Emergency Care: Emergency care is provided in urgent situations such as accidents,sudden illnesses,or 
                traumatic injuries.It aims to stabilize patients and provide immediate treatment to prevent further complications.
                

                Mental Health Care: Thisn branch of healthcare addresses mental illnesses, emotional disorders,and psychological issues.It includes therapy,
                counseling, medication, and support services.Health Insurance: Health insurance helps individuals and families cover the costs of medical expenses.
                It can be provided by private companies,employers,or government programs, depending on the country's healthcare system. Healthcare Technology: Advances
                in technology have revolutionized healthcare delivery, with innovations like telemedicine, electronic health records 
                (EHRs), medical imaging, and wearable devices improving patient care, efficiency, and accessibility.
                Public Health: Public health focuses on promoting and protecting the health of communities through initiatives such as disease surveillance, health 
                education campaigns, sanitation, and environmental regulations.   
                The healthcare system varies from country to country, with differences in funding, organization, and accessibility. Some countries have universal healthcare 
                systems where the government provides healthcare services to all residents, while others rely on a mix of public and private providers. Healthcare policies,
                regulations, and funding mechanisms play a significant role in shaping the delivery and quality of care.
